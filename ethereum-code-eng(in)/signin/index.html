What is Ethereum?

Ethereum is a digital asset born in 2009 as an alternative way to store, trade, and invest your money. Unlike fiat currencies, Ethereum doesn’t exist in any kind of physical form, so you can only access it digitally.

Essentially, Ethereum is an encrypted computer file. When you store Ethereum in your digital wallet, you’re storing computer files with monetary value. You can use your crypto to make international purchases, trade on the market, and more. Ethereum is also a decentralized asset, meaning that it doesn’t have to go through any banks or government entities.

Therefore, people are free to spend their Ethereum in any way they choose, without paying bank fees or going through other regulations. Another thing to keep in mind is that Ethereum is stored on international servers, it is a global currency, and in this digital day and age, it’s vital to have an asset that you can use to pay for anything you want regardless of where you are.

What is an NFT?

Non-fungible tokens (NTFs) allow users to represent unique items as Ethereum-based assets. Basically, they act like a deed and prove that the item you are selling or sharing is, in fact, yours. These tokens can have only one owner at a time, are worth one USD each, and are only created by you.

Unlike paper money, you can’t create new NFTs or bring them into existence unless you own what is being sold. Each NFT is digitally unique and needs to have a verified owner that can be cross-referenced and checked before any trading is done.
